Problem: Given f(x) = x³ - 6x² + 11x - 6, find the remainder when divided by (x - 2).

Solution:

We’ll apply the Remainder Theorem and substitute x = 2 into the function.

f(x) = x³ - 6x² + 11x - 6

Substitute x = 2:

f(2) = (2)³ - 6(2)² + 11(2) - 6

f(2) = 8 - 24 + 22 - 6 = 0

So, the remainder is 0, and (x - 2) is a factor of the polynomial.

The factored form of the polynomial is:

f(x) = (x - 2)(x² - 4x + 3)
